Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about how VaultKey protects your data.

Every vault entry is encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a key derived from your master password via PBKDF2 with 480,000 iterations and a unique random salt. This all happens locally using the browser's native WebCrypto API — your plaintext passwords are never sent anywhere.
Zero-knowledge means VaultKey itself never has access to your master password or unencrypted vault contents. Only an encrypted blob is stored in your browser's local storage — without your master password, that blob is computationally meaningless.
No. There are no servers involved at any point. All analysis, generation, and encryption/decryption runs entirely client-side in your browser. We have no mechanism to view, transmit, or recover your stored credentials.
Your vault cannot be recovered. Because of the zero-knowledge design, there is no password reset path or backdoor — the encryption key is derived solely from your master password. Store it somewhere safe, such as a physical note or another password manager.
No. Your encrypted vault blob is saved only in this browser's local storage on this device. It does not sync to any cloud service. Clearing your browser data or switching devices means the vault stays where it was created.
